Frequently Asked, Reluctantly Answered

We won't sugarcoat it. We didn't sugarcoat anything else.

Why is there no swiping?

Because infinite choice doesn't make you happier — it makes you worse at deciding. Studies on choice overload show people are far less likely to commit when given twenty-four options instead of six. We've done the maths. You get one option. Cherish it.

What is "the Gini Coefficient of Being Fancied"?

An economic measure of inequality, repurposed here for romantic despair. On most dating apps, attention is distributed more unevenly than income in a small, unstable nation. We've flattened the curve. Everyone here is, on average, equally overlooked.

Do you verify people's structural flaws?

We take them at their word, then let reality confirm it within roughly three dates.

What happens after I'm matched?

You receive a short, factual email. No fireworks, no confetti. Something like: "Christian from Cudden also has a bad back and prefers to be in bed by 10:15pm with an audiobook. Your practical compatibility is 84%." Do with that what you will.

Is this a real dating service?

No. SalvageRomance.com is a parody website, not a functioning dating platform. No accounts are created, no data is stored, and no matches are made. Please don't send us photographs, emotional baggage, or money. We have quite enough of both already.

⚠A quick, unfunny word about safety

SalvageRomance.com is satire. There is no real registration on this page — nothing you click here collects or stores anything.

Read the advice

The underlying point is genuine: a domain being mentioned in a YouTube video, podcast, or any piece of online content doesn't mean it's safe. Creators can misspeak a URL, links can be hijacked, sponsored segments aren't security-vetted, and scammers routinely register lookalike domains to ride the traffic of anything that goes viral. "I heard about it in a video" is not verification.

Before you enter personal information anywhere:

  • Check the site independently rather than trusting a single link or mention
  • Look for a proper privacy policy and HTTPS — and don't stop there, both are trivial to fake
  • Be suspicious if a site asks for more than it needs
  • Use a password manager and enable MFA wherever you can
  • If in doubt, don't submit

For further reading: National Cyber Security Centre · Information Commissioner's Office · Cygnet Security for security consulting
